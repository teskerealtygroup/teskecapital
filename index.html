Our Principles

Capital deserves a clear philosophy.

Our objective is not to predict every market move. It is to make thoughtful decisions, remain adaptable, and construct a portfolio capable of participating in long-term growth while maintaining the discipline required during difficult markets.

Research before allocation

Every position should earn its place through a defined investment thesis and an understanding of associated risks.

Patience over noise

We prioritize durable opportunities over short-lived headlines, speculation, and market distractions.

Transparency over hype

Investors deserve direct communication about process, risk, performance, and changing market conditions.

Alignment over volume

We seek compatible investor relationships rather than pursuing assets under management at any cost.

Proposed Fee Structure

Simple, transparent, and aligned.

Teske Capital anticipates charging one straightforward annual management fee based on the value of each investor’s assets under management. The proposed structure is designed to remain clear and easy to understand without unnecessary layers of fees.

Proposed Annual Management Fee 1.00% Of each investor’s assets under management annually

The management fee is intended to compensate Teske Capital for portfolio research, asset allocation, risk monitoring, investment oversight, performance reporting, and ongoing portfolio management.

01

Straightforward calculation

The proposed annual fee equals 1.00% of the applicable value of each investor’s managed capital.

$10,000 managed $100 annually
$120,000 managed $1,200 annually
$250,000 managed $2,500 annually
02

No proposed performance fee

Teske Capital does not currently anticipate charging an incentive or performance fee. The proposed compensation structure is limited to the annual management fee, subject to the final legal and offering documents.

03

Proposed quarterly assessment

The annual fee may be calculated and charged quarterly in arrears, generally representing approximately 0.25% per quarter. The final calculation method will be defined in the governing documents.

04

Full disclosure before investing

Investors will receive complete information regarding management fees, fund expenses, calculation methods, payment timing, and any applicable waivers before committing capital.

This proposed fee structure is presented for development and planning purposes only. Teske Capital is not currently accepting outside investor capital. Final fees, expenses, billing practices, and investor terms will be established in the applicable operating, subscription, offering, and disclosure documents and may differ from the structure described above.

Frequently Asked Questions

Clear answers. No unnecessary complexity.

Learn more about Teske Capital, our proprietary model portfolio, investment philosophy, and plans for future investor access.

What is Teske Capital?
Teske Capital is a private investment firm being developed around disciplined, long-term capital allocation. The strategy evaluates opportunities across public equities, exchange-traded funds, digital assets, Treasury securities, gold-related investments, real estate securities, and cash equivalents.
Is Teske Capital currently accepting investors?
No. Teske Capital is currently managing and documenting a proprietary model portfolio using the founder’s own capital. The firm is not presently accepting outside investor funds. Future access will depend on completion of the appropriate business, legal, regulatory, and operational requirements.
What types of assets may the portfolio hold?
The investment universe may include Bitcoin, Ethereum, broad-market and sector ETFs, Berkshire Hathaway, Realty Income, Treasury Bills, gold ETFs, cash equivalents, and other investments consistent with the firm’s strategy. Holdings and allocations may change as valuations, economic conditions, and market risks evolve.
Does Teske Capital guarantee investment returns?
No. No return, yield, annual percentage, or investment outcome is guaranteed. All investments involve risk, including the possible loss of principal. Historical and model-portfolio results do not guarantee future performance.
How often is portfolio performance updated?
The proprietary model portfolio is generally updated weekly. Teske Capital uses weekly reporting to provide a consistent view of NAV, portfolio value, gains or losses, and investment performance without placing excessive emphasis on daily market noise.
What does NAV mean?
NAV means net asset value. On this website, the model NAV begins at $100.00 per model unit and moves in proportion to the portfolio’s performance. For example, a NAV of $105.60 represents a 5.60% increase from the initial $100.00 model value.
How does Teske Capital manage risk?
Risk management may include diversification, position sizing, maintaining cash reserves, rebalancing among asset classes, monitoring liquidity, and increasing exposure to defensive holdings such as Treasury securities and gold during periods of elevated uncertainty.
Why does the strategy include both equities and digital assets?
Teske Capital uses a multi-asset framework designed to participate in different sources of long-term growth. Public equities and ETFs provide exposure to established businesses and broad markets, while Bitcoin and Ethereum may offer exposure to developing digital financial infrastructure. Digital-asset allocations may be reduced when market, liquidity, or economic risks increase.
Where can I read the firm’s investment policy?
The current Investment Policy Statement explains the firm’s objectives, eligible assets, allocation approach, liquidity policy, and risk-management principles. Read the Policy Statement →
How can I express interest in investing later?
A dedicated contact page will allow prospective investors to introduce themselves and request additional information. Submitting an inquiry will not constitute an investment commitment, subscription, offer, or acceptance by Teske Capital.
