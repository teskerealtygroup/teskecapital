Teske Capital Strategy

Our Investment Universe.

Teske Capital allocates across a defined group of assets selected for growth, diversification, income, liquidity, inflation protection, and resilience during changing market conditions.

Multi-Asset Investment Framework
01 Long-Term Growth

Participate in the growth of public companies, technology, and developing digital markets.

02 Diversification

Reduce dependence on one company, sector, asset class, or economic outcome.

03 Income and Liquidity

Generate portfolio income while maintaining capital available for future opportunities.

04 Defensive Resilience

Maintain assets intended to help during inflation, volatility, and economic uncertainty.

Purpose-Driven Allocation

Every asset should have a reason to exist.

Teske Capital does not seek to own assets merely because they are popular. Each position should serve a defined purpose within the portfolio, whether that purpose is growth, income, liquidity, diversification, inflation protection, or downside resilience. Holdings and allocations may change as valuations, risks, and economic conditions evolve.

Category 01

Core Market Exposure

Broad-market funds provide a diversified foundation and reduce the need to depend entirely on individual stock selection.

VOO Core Holding

Broad-Market ETF

Vanguard S&P 500 ETF

VOO provides diversified exposure to many of the largest and most established public companies in the United States. It serves as a foundation for long-term participation in the broader U.S. economy.

Why it exists

Core market participation, broad diversification, and long-term capital appreciation.

Category 02

Technology and Growth

These funds provide targeted exposure to innovation, digital infrastructure, and businesses capable of producing above-average long-term growth.

QQQ Growth

Nasdaq-100 ETF

Invesco QQQ Trust

QQQ provides exposure to many of the largest nonfinancial companies listed on the Nasdaq. Its holdings are concentrated in technology, communications, consumer growth, and other innovation-led industries.

Why it exists

Participation in innovative companies with meaningful long-term growth potential.

VGT Sector Growth

Technology ETF

Vanguard Information Technology ETF

VGT provides focused exposure to companies involved in software, semiconductors, hardware, cloud infrastructure, cybersecurity, and other areas of information technology.

Why it exists

Targeted participation in the technology sector and the continued expansion of digital infrastructure.

Category 03

Sector Diversification

Sector funds broaden the portfolio beyond technology and allow capital to participate in different parts of the economy.

VFH Cyclical

Financials ETF

Vanguard Financials ETF

VFH provides exposure to banks, insurers, investment firms, payment businesses, and other financial-service companies.

Why it exists

Diversification beyond technology and participation in credit, banking, insurance, and financial activity.

VHT Defensive Growth

Health Care ETF

Vanguard Health Care ETF

VHT provides exposure to pharmaceutical companies, medical technology, biotechnology, health services, and other businesses tied to long-term health-care demand.

Why it exists

A combination of defensive demand, innovation, and long-term demographic growth.

VIS Economic Growth

Industrials ETF

Vanguard Industrials ETF

VIS provides exposure to industrial companies involved in aerospace, transportation, construction, machinery, logistics, and infrastructure.

Why it exists

Participation in manufacturing, infrastructure spending, transportation, and broader economic expansion.

VPU Defensive Income

Utilities ETF

Vanguard Utilities ETF

VPU provides exposure to electricity, natural gas, water, and utility infrastructure companies whose services remain necessary across economic cycles.

Why it exists

Defensive exposure, dividend income, and reduced dependence on high-growth market sectors.

Category 04

Quality Public Equity

Select individual companies may be held when their structure, leadership, assets, and capital-allocation discipline provide a compelling long-term case.

BRK.B Quality Compounder

Public Equity

Berkshire Hathaway

Berkshire Hathaway owns a collection of operating businesses, insurance companies, public securities, and cash-producing assets. Its structure offers internal diversification and a history of disciplined capital allocation.

Why it exists

Exposure to durable businesses, insurance operations, financial strength, and experienced capital allocation.

Category 05

Digital Assets

Digital assets provide exposure to a developing financial and technological ecosystem, but allocations may be actively reduced when volatility, liquidity, or economic risk increases.

BTC Alternative Growth

Digital Asset

Bitcoin

Bitcoin is a scarce digital asset with a fixed issuance framework and a globally accessible network. Teske Capital views it as a potential long-term store of value and an alternative asset with asymmetric growth potential.

Why it exists

Long-term digital-asset exposure, monetary diversification, and potential upside outside traditional financial markets.

ETH Digital Infrastructure

Digital Asset

Ethereum

Ethereum provides infrastructure for smart contracts, decentralized applications, tokenized assets, and digital financial services. Its value proposition is tied to the continued use and development of its network.

Why it exists

Exposure to blockchain infrastructure and the potential development of programmable digital finance.

Category 06

Income and Real Estate

Income-producing assets can support portfolio cash flow and provide exposure to sources of return beyond equity price appreciation.

O Income

Real Estate Investment Trust

Realty Income

Realty Income owns a diversified portfolio of commercial properties leased to operating businesses. It is known for distributing dividends on a monthly schedule.

Why it exists

Real-estate exposure, recurring dividend income, and diversification beyond stocks, bonds, and digital assets.

Category 07

Defensive Assets and Liquidity

Defensive assets are intended to preserve flexibility and help the portfolio withstand inflation, volatility, geopolitical uncertainty, and market downturns.

T Capital Preservation

Treasury Securities

Treasury Bills

Treasury Bills are short-duration obligations of the United States government. They may provide income while helping maintain liquidity and limit exposure to longer-duration interest-rate risk.

Why they exist

Liquidity, capital preservation, short-duration income, and dry powder for future opportunities.

Au Inflation Hedge

Alternative Exposure

Gold ETFs

Gold ETFs provide exposure to the price of gold without requiring the direct storage of physical bullion. Gold may behave differently from equities during periods of inflation, currency concern, or geopolitical stress.

Why they exist

Inflation sensitivity, geopolitical diversification, and defensive exposure during periods of reduced market confidence.

CASH Liquidity Reserve

Cash and Cash Equivalents

Strategic Cash Reserves

Cash is not treated as a failure to invest. It provides stability, flexibility, and the ability to act when market prices become more attractive or when risk increases.

Why it exists

Teske Capital intends to generally maintain a meaningful liquidity reserve, currently expected to fall near 10% to 20% depending on market conditions.

Allocation Philosophy

The universe is defined. The allocation remains flexible.

Teske Capital may adjust the weight of each asset as valuations, inflation, interest rates, geopolitical risks, market sentiment, and economic conditions change. The goal is not to maintain a permanently fixed portfolio. It is to remain disciplined while allowing the portfolio to evolve.

01

Increase growth exposure selectively

Technology, broad-market equities, and digital assets may receive greater allocation when valuations, liquidity, and market conditions support additional risk.

02

Increase defensive exposure when needed

Treasury securities, gold, utilities, and cash reserves may be increased during inflation, geopolitical stress, or elevated uncertainty.

03

Reduce concentration risk

Sector funds, real estate, quality public equities, and defensive assets help reduce dependence on technology or any single source of return.

04

Preserve liquidity

Cash and short-duration Treasury securities allow the portfolio to respond to market opportunities without being forced to sell long-term holdings at unfavorable prices.
