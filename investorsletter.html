2024

Stated Annual Return +9.80%

The 2024 draft archive reflects a broadly diversified growth strategy with technology and broad-market exposure supported by Treasury securities, defensive sectors, and selective digital-asset participation.

Q1 2024
Establishing disciplined growth exposure January through March 2024
+2.10% +

The first quarter focused on maintaining exposure to broad U.S. equities and technology while avoiding excessive concentration in any single company. VOO, QQQ, and VGT represented the portfolio’s principal growth engines, while sector exposure helped reduce dependence on one market theme.

Treasury Bills and cash equivalents remained important sources of liquidity. Rather than treating idle cash as unproductive, short-duration government securities provided income and preserved flexibility for future allocation changes.

Quarter focus

  • Core exposure through VOO, QQQ, and VGT.
  • Liquidity maintained through Treasury Bills and cash.
  • Controlled digital-asset exposure through Bitcoin and Ethereum.

Sincerely, Teddy R. Teske Founder & Portfolio Manager

Expanding diversification beyond technology April through June 2024
+2.40% +

Q3 2024
Balancing conviction with defensive reserves July through September 2024
+2.70% +

The third quarter emphasized maintaining participation in long-term growth while preserving the ability to respond to volatility. Technology and broad-market positions remained central, but liquidity and defensive assets continued to serve as stabilizing components.

Gold-related exposure was viewed as a portfolio diversifier rather than a short-term trade. The objective was not to predict macroeconomic events, but to maintain assets that could behave differently during inflation, geopolitical stress, or periods of falling confidence.

Q4 2024
Finishing the year with balanced participation October through December 2024
+2.30% +

The final quarter of 2024 was approached with an emphasis on preserving gains without abandoning assets supported by long-term conviction. Broad-market and technology exposure remained meaningful, while sector ETFs, Treasury Bills, gold, and income assets helped maintain a more balanced risk profile.

The year reinforced the value of combining growth and defensive assets rather than attempting to identify a single permanent winner. The portfolio finished 2024 with a stated annual gain of 9.80%.

Year-end observations

  • Liquidity supported patience and flexibility.
