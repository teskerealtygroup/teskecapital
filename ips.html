Section 01

Purpose of this policy

This Investment Policy Statement establishes the operating framework for the Teske Capital proprietary model portfolio. It is intended to promote consistency, discipline, and accountability in investment decisions while allowing sufficient flexibility to respond to changing market, economic, and geopolitical conditions.

Section 02

Investment objective

The primary objective of Teske Capital is to pursue long-term capital appreciation while managing downside exposure through diversification, liquidity, position sizing, and the strategic use of defensive assets. The portfolio seeks to participate in periods of economic expansion while remaining positioned to withstand inflation, elevated market volatility, geopolitical conflict, recessionary conditions, and prolonged market downturns.

Teske Capital does not seek to guarantee a fixed return or annual yield. Performance objectives are pursued over full market cycles, and short-term results may vary materially from long-term expectations.

Section 03

Eligible investment universe

The portfolio may invest across public equities, broad-market exchange-traded funds, sector-specific ETFs, digital assets, Treasury securities, gold-related instruments, real estate investment trusts, money-market instruments, and cash equivalents. Eligible holdings may include Bitcoin, Ethereum, VOO, QQQ, VGT, VFH, VHT, VIS, VPU, Berkshire Hathaway, Realty Income, Treasury Bills, and gold ETFs.

Section 04

Dynamic allocation and rebalancing

Teske Capital follows a flexible allocation process rather than maintaining fixed percentages across every asset category. Portfolio weights may be adjusted in response to economic growth expectations, inflation, interest rates, liquidity conditions, valuation, market leadership, volatility, and geopolitical risk.

During periods characterized by strong economic growth, improving liquidity, and positive risk sentiment, the portfolio may increase exposure to technology, growth equities, and digital assets. When market leadership weakens or economic uncertainty rises, capital may be reallocated toward Treasury securities, gold, utilities, healthcare, cash equivalents, and other defensive holdings. Rebalancing is performed when the underlying economic or investment thesis changes—not merely because of short-term price fluctuations.

Section 05

Role of Treasury securities and gold

Treasury Bills and gold-related investments serve an important role in capital preservation, liquidity management, and portfolio diversification. Their allocation may be increased during periods of elevated inflation, monetary uncertainty, geopolitical conflict, weakening economic conditions, or excessive equity-market volatility.

These assets are not viewed solely as passive holdings. They are part of an active risk-management framework designed to reduce dependence on a single market environment and preserve capital that may later be redeployed into higher-growth opportunities.

Section 06

Digital-asset allocation

Bitcoin and Ethereum may provide long-term growth potential, monetary diversification, and exposure to developing digital financial infrastructure. However, digital assets may also experience substantial volatility, liquidity stress, regulatory uncertainty, and severe drawdowns.

Teske Capital may reduce digital-asset exposure during periods of heightened fear, tightening financial conditions, geopolitical conflict, persistent inflation, or declining market liquidity. Repositioning may begin when risk conditions improve, market sentiment stabilizes, and indicators such as the Crypto Fear & Greed Index move from extreme fear toward neutral or constructive conditions. No single sentiment indicator will determine allocation decisions by itself; it will be evaluated alongside price structure, liquidity, macroeconomic data, and broader market conditions.

Section 07

Cash reserves and liquidity

Teske Capital intends to maintain sufficient liquidity to meet operating needs, withstand volatility, and take advantage of opportunities created by market dislocations. The portfolio will generally target cash and cash-equivalent reserves of 10% to 20% of total proprietary assets.

Normal Liquidity Baseline Approximately 15%

The actual allocation may move below or above the normal baseline when supported by market conditions, pending opportunities, portfolio risk, or capital requirements.

Section 08

Risk-management principles

Portfolio risk will be managed through diversification, liquidity, position sizing, asset-class rotation, periodic rebalancing, and ongoing review of each investment thesis. Teske Capital will seek to avoid excessive dependence on any single security, sector, asset class, economic outcome, or source of return.

Capital preservation does not mean avoiding all losses. Market declines are an inherent part of investing. The objective is to prevent temporary market volatility from becoming permanent impairment through disciplined allocation, sufficient liquidity, and a willingness to reduce exposure when risk materially exceeds the expected reward.

Section 09

Monitoring and policy review

Portfolio holdings and market conditions will be monitored continuously, with performance recorded on a weekly basis. Allocation, liquidity, risk exposure, and investment theses will be reviewed regularly. This Investment Policy Statement will receive a formal review at least annually and may be amended as the firm develops, economic conditions evolve, or the legal structure of Teske Capital changes.
